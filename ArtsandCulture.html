Arts and Culture

Waterford Walls

Waterford walls is a collection of graffiti paintings around Waterford. The painted walls brighten everything up developing Waterford as a city. You can get a guided tour that brings you to over 60 murals.

Waterford Crystal

Waterford crystal is a huge tourist attraction with a lot of tourists visiting daily. Waterford crystal was founded in 1783 but glass itself has a huge heritage in Waterford, evidence suggesting it goes back as far as 508BC. They have created iconic crystal pieces such as the PGA tour of America trophy.

Viking Triangle

The Viking triangle is probably the best way to see Waterford as it shows 1100 years of history at different points and places across Waterford. You will see Waterford ‘vedraford’ what the Vikings used to call it up the Victorians gaining a vast amount of knowledge and insight to the city.

Bishops Palace

Bishops Palace was home to Anglican bishops in Waterford from 1743 to 1919. This beautiful piece of architecture is now a museum it displays some of the finest pieces of history from Georgian and Victorian times.

Garter Lane

Garter Lane is a wonderful little theatre that also has modern art for display. Plays are regularly played and is highly recommended if you enjoy the odd play or two.

Blue Plaques

Waterford walls is a trail of blue plaques that link locations around the city to commemorate a person or event. It is a most in depth and interesting way of seeing Waterford.
